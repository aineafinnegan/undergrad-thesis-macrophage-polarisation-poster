# Generating a gene marker panel to identify murine macrophage polarisation subtypes

Undergraduate research poster exploring murine macrophage polarisation, with a focus on identifying markers of M1 and M2 phenotypes and investigating preliminary hybrid macrophage states.

## Overview
This project examined macrophage polarisation in vitro to assess which gene and soluble markers could reliably distinguish classical M1 and M2 phenotypes in murine macrophages. The work also explored early findings in hybrid macrophage states generated through priming approaches.

## Project aims
- Identify markers associated with M1 macrophages
- Identify markers associated with M2 macrophages
- Explore preliminary evidence for hybrid macrophage phenotypes
- Assess how priming may influence macrophage functional states

## Methods
The project used a range of experimental and molecular techniques, including:
- cell culture
- RNA isolation
- RT-qPCR
- Western blotting
- Griess assay
- ELISA

## Key findings
- M1 markers identified included CD38 and nitrite secretion
- iNOS/NOS2 showed promise as an M1 marker, although additional repeats were needed
- M2 markers identified included Ym1, FIZZ1, c-Myc, and IL-10 secretion
- Arg1 was not confirmed as an M2 marker in this study
- Preliminary hybrid macrophage findings suggested that priming may produce more functionally distinct phenotypes

## Future directions
- Repeat hybrid experiments with a broader marker panel
- Include peritoneal macrophages alongside bone marrow-derived macrophages
- Investigate additional M2 subsets
- Use flow cytometry for single-cell surface protein analysis
